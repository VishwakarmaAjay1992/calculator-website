Why use Wealth Planner

Most online calculators handle one number and stop there. Wealth Planner is built to go a step further: every tool shows the full year-by-year (and month-by-month) breakdown behind the headline figure, so you can see exactly how your money grows or draws down — not just the final total. Each calculator also comes with practical, India-specific guidance: tax rules, FOIR limits, eligibility criteria for government schemes, and the trade-offs lenders and banks don't always spell out.

Every result can be exported to a formatted Excel workbook in one click, so you can take the schedule into your own budgeting, share it with a family member, or hand it to your financial advisor.

Frequently asked questions

Is Wealth Planner free to use?

Yes. All six calculators are completely free, with no signup required. Estimates are for educational purposes and are not financial advice.

Which calculator should I start with?

If you are building wealth, start with the SIP calculator. If you are planning to draw an income from savings, use SWP. For a loan decision, use the Loan EMI calculator, and for retirement planning, use the dedicated Retirement calculator, which ties SIP and SWP logic together.

Are the government scheme interest rates current?

Rates for PPF, Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana, SCSS, NSC, KVP and POMIS are reviewed by the Ministry of Finance every quarter. This tool uses the latest published rates at the time of writing but you should always confirm the current rate before making a decision.

This tool provides general, educational estimates only — it is not financial, tax, or investment advice. Actual returns, interest rates, and tax rules vary and change over time; please verify current rates and consult a certified financial advisor or chartered accountant before making decisions.
