    India's small savings schemes are sovereign-backed, meaning the government guarantees the return — no market risk. Rates are reviewed by the Ministry of Finance every quarter and have stayed unusually stable in recent years, but each scheme has its own compounding rule, tenure, and eligibility criteria, which this calculator models individually rather than treating them as interchangeable.

    Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY)

    Built specifically for a girl child's future, SSY currently offers the highest rate among small savings schemes and is fully tax-free (EEE status). Deposits are made for the first 15 years; the balance then continues compounding untouched until the account matures 21 years after opening.

    Senior Citizens' Savings Scheme (SCSS)

    Designed for retirement income, SCSS pays interest out quarterly rather than compounding it — meaning your principal itself doesn't grow, but you receive a steady, government-guaranteed income stream every quarter, fully taxable at your slab rate.

    National Savings Certificate (NSC)

    A simple 5-year, annually compounding certificate. The accrued interest each year (except the final year) is deemed reinvested and also qualifies for a Section 80C deduction, which can offset the fact that this "phantom income" is taxable even though you receive nothing until maturity.

    Kisan Vikas Patra (KVP)

    KVP's entire appeal is simplicity: your investment doubles in a fixed, government-notified period (currently around 115 months) with no tax deduction on the investment, and fully taxable interest.

    Post Office Monthly Income Scheme (POMIS)

    Similar in spirit to SCSS but open to any adult, not just seniors — POMIS pays a fixed monthly income on a 5-year deposit, capped at ₹9 lakh for a single account or ₹15 lakh jointly.

    Frequently asked questions

    Are these small savings scheme rates fixed for the full tenure?

    It depends on the scheme. PPF, SCSS, and SSY rates can be revised each quarter by the government (though your accrued balance keeps its already-earned interest). NSC and KVP rates are locked in at the time of purchase and don't change for that certificate's full term, even if the rate for new purchases changes later.

    Which scheme has the highest current interest rate?

    As of the most recent quarter reflected in this calculator, Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana and the Senior Citizens' Savings Scheme share the highest rate among the major small savings schemes — but SSY is restricted to a girl child's account, and SCSS is restricted to those 60 and above (55+ for certain retirees).

    Do any of these schemes offer tax-free returns?

    PPF and Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana both carry full EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt) tax status — contribution, interest, and maturity amount are all tax-free. SCSS, NSC, KVP, and POMIS interest is taxable, though NSC's reinvested interest can still count toward your Section 80C limit.
