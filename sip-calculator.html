A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) lets you invest a fixed amount into a mutual fund every month, rather than as one lump sum. Because you buy units regularly regardless of price, a SIP naturally averages out your purchase cost over market ups and downs — a discipline that matters more than timing the market for most long-term investors.

How this SIP calculator works

The calculator compounds your monthly contribution using the standard SIP (annuity due) formula, where each month's investment is assumed to happen at the start of the month:

FV = P × [((1+i)^n − 1) / i] × (1+i)

Where P is your monthly investment, i is the monthly rate of return (annual return ÷ 12), and n is the number of months. If you enable an annual step-up, your contribution amount increases by that percentage every 12 months, which is modeled month by month rather than as an approximation.

What counts as a realistic return assumption?

Equity mutual funds in India have historically delivered long-term annualized returns in the broad range of 10–14%, though any specific number is never guaranteed and past performance does not predict future results. Hybrid and debt funds typically run lower and steadier. It's worth running the calculator at a conservative, moderate, and optimistic rate to see the spread of outcomes rather than anchoring on a single number.

Why step-up SIPs matter

A flat monthly SIP ignores the fact that your income usually grows over your career. A step-up SIP — increasing your contribution by, say, 10% every year in line with a typical raise — can meaningfully shorten the time it takes to reach a goal, without feeling like a bigger sacrifice at any single point in time, since each increase is timed to your income growth.

Frequently asked questions

Is SIP better than a lump sum investment?

Neither is universally better — a SIP reduces timing risk by spreading purchases over time, which suits ongoing income and volatile markets. A lump sum can outperform if invested right before a sustained rally, but carries more timing risk. Many investors use both: a SIP for ongoing savings and a lump sum for windfalls like a bonus.

Can I stop a SIP anytime?

Yes, SIPs in mutual funds can typically be paused or stopped anytime without penalty, though your existing units stay invested and keep growing or falling with the market until you redeem them.

What is a good SIP step-up percentage?

A common approach is to match your expected annual salary increment, often 8–12% for a salaried professional in India, so your investment grows in step with your income rather than becoming a larger share of your budget each year.
